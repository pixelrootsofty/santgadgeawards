Program 05 — Education

Essay Competitions

Promoting critical thinking and civic awareness among students through essays on the issues that will shape India's next decade.

Writing as a Civic Act

Our annual essay competition is open to school and college students across Bengal. The brief is simple — pick one of four national topics, write 800 to 1,200 words in any language you are comfortable in, and tell us what you actually think.

Entries are judged blind by a panel of teachers, journalists and senior public servants. The strongest essays are published in the award souvenir and read aloud at the felicitation ceremony.

Topics Students Engage With

We pick topics that matter to the country, not topics that fit neatly into a syllabus. Students may write in English, Hindi or Bengali.

  • Social responsibility — what does it mean to be a citizen, and not just a voter?
  • Clean India Mission — Sant Gadge Maharaj's vision in a 21st-century city
  • Environmental awareness — climate, rivers, air, and the cost of inaction
  • Role of youth in nation building — what can a 17-year-old actually change?

How We Run the Competition

Three Languages

Write in English, Hindi or Bengali — your voice, your tongue.

Blind Judging

Names removed before evaluation — only the writing speaks.

Published

Top essays printed in the official award souvenir.

Read Aloud

Winning students share their work at the ceremony.
