Program 04 — Children

Drawing Competitions

Encouraging creativity in children and youth, while spreading social awareness through the universal language of art.

When Children Draw, Society Listens

Our annual drawing competition invites students from primary and secondary schools across Bengal to put their visions of a better society on paper. The themes are deliberately serious — environment, harmony, cleanliness — but the canvases are wonderfully unfiltered.

Selected entries travel as a touring exhibition to schools, government offices and the main award ceremony, where the young artists are felicitated alongside doctors, soldiers and social workers.

Themes Our Young Artists Explore

Children receive a free art kit on arrival, two hours of quiet space, and a panel of artists and educators to judge — but no prescriptive answers.

  • Environment — climate, rivers, forests and the world we want to inherit
  • Social harmony — friendship across faiths, languages and backgrounds
  • Cleanliness — Sant Gadge Maharaj's lifelong message, in every brushstroke
  • Community welfare — heroes, helpers and the people who make a neighbourhood feel like home
