Award Category 02

Social Workers

Individuals actively engaged in social development — helping underprivileged communities, leading awareness campaigns, and shaping a more compassionate society.

The Quiet Force Behind Every Community

For every visible milestone of progress, there are dozens of social workers whose names rarely appear in headlines. They are the volunteers organising relief after a flood, the campaigners knocking on doors to spread awareness, and the neighbourhood organisers turning small gatherings into movements.

The Sant Gadge Seva Award celebrates these individuals — citizens whose work directly improves the lives of underprivileged communities and inspires others to follow. Their service is rooted in the same teachings of cleanliness, compassion, and public welfare that Sant Gadge Maharaj championed throughout his life.

Four Pillars of Social Work

Community Support

Programs that strengthen neighborhoods through food drives, education access, and elder care initiatives.

Awareness Campaigns

Door-to-door and digital outreach on public health, sanitation, women's rights, and environmental responsibility.

Relief Activities

Disaster response, blood donation drives, oxygen and medicine distribution, and emergency assistance to families in need.

Public Welfare Initiatives

Long-term projects in education, livelihood, healthcare access, and environmental protection across Bengal.

Service to humanity is service to the divine. The social worker who quietly cleans a street, teaches a child, or comforts a stranger carries forward the legacy of Sant Gadge Maharaj.
— From the Award Committee

Programs Led by Our Awardees

Durga Puja Community Programs

During the Durga Puja celebrations, our social workers organise community programs that bring neighbourhoods together and spread messages of unity, culture, and harmony.

  • Community gatherings and feasts
  • Cultural participation across age groups
  • Social awareness initiatives during Sarad Samman

Plantation Drives

Tree plantation campaigns are organised every monsoon to promote environmental awareness and encourage citizens to contribute to a greener, healthier Bengal.

  • Promote environmental protection
  • Encourage community participation
  • Spread awareness about climate responsibility

Women's Day Celebrations

Special events organised every March 8 honour women's achievements and promote gender equality through felicitation ceremonies and discussions.

  • Women empowerment discussions
  • Felicitation of women achievers
  • Awareness programs on rights and safety

Drawing & Essay Competitions

Annual student competitions encourage creativity and critical thinking on themes of social responsibility, cleanliness, and nation-building.

  • Themes: environment, harmony, Clean India
  • Open to schools across multiple districts
  • Awarded works are showcased at the ceremony
