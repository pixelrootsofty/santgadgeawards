Award Category 01

Corona Warriors

Frontline heroes who played a crucial role during the COVID-19 pandemic — protecting citizens, saving lives, and ensuring public safety when the nation needed them most.

Standing as the Nation's Shield

The COVID-19 pandemic tested India like no other crisis in recent memory. While much of the country sheltered indoors, an extraordinary group of professionals stepped forward — into hospital wards, onto deserted streets, and into communities they had sworn to protect.

The Sant Gadge Seva Award honors these Corona Warriors: the doctors who worked back-to-back shifts in PPE, the nurses who held the hands of dying patients, the police and paramilitary forces who maintained order with empathy, and the medical staff whose names were never in headlines but whose service saved countless lives.

Honoring Six Categories of Frontline Service

NDRF Personnel

National Disaster Response Force teams who deployed across Bengal for relief, evacuation, and emergency response.

RPF Personnel

Railway Protection Force officers who kept stations safe, delivered Shramik trains, and supported migrant workers.

Police Officers

Constables and senior officers who enforced safety protocols while distributing food and essentials to the vulnerable.

AIIMS Doctors

Specialists and residents from AIIMS who led ICU care, set treatment protocols, and trained frontline medics.

Medical Staff

Nurses, lab technicians, ward boys, and ambulance crews — the backbone of every hospital during the surge.

Volunteer Responders

Citizens who stepped up to coordinate oxygen, plasma, and food during the most difficult waves of the pandemic.

They worked tirelessly, without rest and without recognition, to protect citizens and ensure public safety during the darkest days of the pandemic. The Sant Gadge Seva Award is our humble offering of gratitude.
— From the Award Committee
